# Building a Quant Community: Notes from IITB

A working document on what a campus quant community can actually deliver — and what we'd like to learn from sister institutions like IIT Kharagpur, IIT Delhi, and IIT Madras. This is a placeholder for an honest comparison; please open a PR if you have first-hand experience at any of these campuses.

## Why this matters

Across IITs, "quant interest" is high but "quant readiness" is patchy. A working community fills three gaps the curriculum doesn't:

1. **A roadmap that goes from zero to interview-ready** — most students learn quant from disjointed Coursera courses and Reddit threads.
2. **Mentor access** — alumni in the field who'll actually answer a DM.
3. **Practice rigour** — peer-reviewed projects, mock interviews, and weekly problems.

## What we want to learn

- How does IIT Kharagpur's *Finance Club* run paper-reading sessions?
- How does IIT Delhi's *Consult Club* coordinate placement prep?
- What are the pitfalls of running a finance club at a tech-heavy campus?

## What we plan to ship at IITB

- Weekly office hours with senior students working in the field.
- A peer-reviewed project pipeline — every project gets at least one sanity-check before it lands on the site.
- A "no-fluff" reading list — every entry has a one-line reason why it's on the list.

If you have first-hand notes from another campus, please contribute. This page becomes more useful with every honest comparison.
