Quant Starter Kit: 7-Day Roadmap

A structured one-week plan to build the math, stats, and code foundations.

Full markdown version: roadmap.md.

  1. Day 1 — What is Quantitative Finance?

    Understand what quants actually do (sell-side vs buy-side, HFT vs systematic), and why math + code beats intuition at scale.

    Action: Write 200 words on the kind of quant role that excites you and why.

  2. Day 2 — Probability & Statistics

    Random variables, expectation, variance, conditional probability, the CLT, and hypothesis testing.

    Action: Solve 5 problems on conditional expectation and Bayes’ theorem.

  3. Day 3 — Linear Algebra

    Vectors, matrices, eigenvalues, PCA. Why portfolios are eigen-decompositions in disguise.

    Action: Compute the principal components of any 5-asset return matrix in NumPy.

  4. Day 4 — Time Series Analysis

    Stationarity, autocorrelation, ARIMA, GARCH. The pitfalls of look-ahead bias and survivorship bias.

    Action: Plot the rolling volatility of NIFTY 50 returns and reflect on regime changes.

  5. Day 5 — Financial Modeling

    Present value, no-arbitrage, Black–Scholes intuition, Greeks.

    Action: Implement a Black–Scholes pricer in Python and chart delta vs spot.

  6. Day 6 — Machine Learning in Finance

    Why naive ML fails on markets. Cross-validation in time series, feature engineering, and avoiding overfitting.

    Action: Build a logistic-regression next-day direction predictor and compare to a coin flip.

  7. Day 7 — Algorithmic Trading

    Strategy lifecycle: idea → backtest → risk → execution. Slippage, fees, and why most backtests lie.

    Action: Backtest a simple moving-average crossover strategy with realistic fees.

Quant Finance — what to read next

Hull’s Options, Futures and Other Derivatives; Wilmott’s introductory volume; Lopez de Prado’s Advances in Financial Machine Learning.

Math for Quant

Strang for linear algebra; Ross for probability; Shreve’s Volume I for stochastic calc when you’re ready.

ML in Finance

Beware leakage. Always validate temporally. Read Lopez de Prado on combinatorial purged k-fold.

Algo Trading

Start with vectorised backtests; graduate to event-driven systems only when you have a real edge to test.
